## Figure 1: Delivery van trajectory

**Placement:** Section 5, *Integration*, after the worked example.

**Description:** A graph of $d(t) = 60t - 5t^2$ for $0 \leq t \leq 12$. Downward parabola with vertex at $(6, 180)$ and zeros at $t = 0$ and $t = 12$. Two secant lines drawn:
- From $(0, 0)$ to $(3, 135)$, with slope $45$ labeled.
- From $(3, 135)$ to $(6, 180)$, with slope $15$ labeled.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should see that the slope of the secant line is the average rate of change, and that as the van approaches the turnaround, the average rate decreases.

**Style notes:** Clean parabola, two secant lines in distinct colors, labeled slopes. Width: full text-block.

## Figure 2 (candidate, not in current draft): The toolkit functions

**Placement:** Section 2, *Concept 1*, after the toolkit functions table.

**Description:** A 3×3 grid showing the eight toolkit functions on small Cartesian axes:
- Constant: $f(x) = 1$ (horizontal line)
- Identity: $f(x) = x$ (line through origin)
- Linear (general): $f(x) = 2x + 1$
- Quadratic: $f(x) = x^2$
- Cubic: $f(x) = x^3$
- Square root: $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$
- Reciprocal: $f(x) = 1/x$
- Absolute value: $f(x) = |x|$

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should be able to recognize each shape at a glance.

**Style notes:** Small, consistent axes for each function. Width: full text-block.

## Figure 3 (candidate, not in current draft): Transformation gallery

**Placement:** Section 4, *Concept 3*, after the transformations subsection.

**Description:** A grid showing $f(x) = x^2$ as the parent function, alongside several transformations:
- $f(x) + 3$: shifted up.
- $f(x - 2)$: shifted right.
- $-f(x)$: reflected.
- $2f(x)$: vertically stretched.
- $f(2x)$: horizontally compressed.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should see each transformation's effect visually.

**Style notes:** Small consistent axes. Width: full text-block.
