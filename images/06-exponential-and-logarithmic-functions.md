## Figure 1: Bacterial growth curve

**Placement:** Section 5, *Integration*, after the worked example.

**Description:** Graph of $P(t) = 500e^{0.275t}$ from $t = 0$ to $t = 12$. Curve passes through $(0, 500)$, $(4, 1500)$, and $(10.9, 10000)$ — all marked. Vertical lines from each marked point dropping to the t-axis.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should notice the rapid acceleration — slow at first, then increasingly steep.

## Figure 2 (candidate): Exponential vs. logarithm as inverses

**Placement:** Section 3, *Concept 2*, after the inverse-function discussion.

**Description:** Graph showing $y = 2^x$ and $y = \log_2(x)$ on the same axes. Line $y = x$ shown as the axis of reflection. Each curve is the mirror image of the other across this line.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Visual proof that logarithms are inverses of exponentials.

## Figure 3 (candidate): Logistic growth curve

**Placement:** Section 4, *Concept 3*, in the logistic growth subsection.

**Description:** S-shaped logistic curve approaching a horizontal asymptote at the carrying capacity. Compared to an exponential curve (which keeps growing) for the same initial conditions.
