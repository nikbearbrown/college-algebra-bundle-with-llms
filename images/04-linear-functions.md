## Figure 1: Nail sales scatter plot with best-fit line

**Placement:** Section 5, *Integration*, after the worked example.

**Description:** Scatter plot of four data points $(30, 110), (45, 145), (60, 175), (75, 210)$ with a best-fit line $S = 2.22T + 43.3$ drawn through them. The line extends from about $T = 25$ to $T = 80$. Dashed segments at the ends ($T < 30$ and $T > 75$) indicate extrapolation regions where the model is less reliable.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should notice that the data points lie close to but not exactly on the line, and that the dashed extrapolation regions warn against trusting the model outside the data range.

**Style notes:** Clean scatter plot with labeled axes, distinct treatment of solid (interpolation) vs. dashed (extrapolation) line segments. Width: full text-block.

## Figure 2 (candidate, not in current draft): Three equation forms of a line

**Placement:** Section 2, *Concept 1*, after introducing the three forms.

**Description:** Three side-by-side panels showing the same line, each labeled with one of the three forms:
- *Slope-intercept:* $y = 3x - 1$
- *Point-slope:* $y - 5 = 3(x - 2)$
- *Standard:* $3x - y = 1$

Each panel highlights what the form makes easy to read off: slope and intercept; slope and point; intercepts.

**Style notes:** Three identical line graphs, callouts in each panel indicating what the form makes visible. Width: full text-block.
