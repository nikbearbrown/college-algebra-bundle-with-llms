## Figure 1: Garden optimization (rectangle + parabola)

**Placement:** Section 5, *Integration*, after the worked example.

**Description:** Two-panel figure.

*Top panel:* a rectangle drawn against a horizontal line representing the house. Two perpendicular sides labeled $x$. The parallel side labeled $40 - 2x$. Inside the rectangle: $A = x(40 - 2x)$.

*Bottom panel:* a graph of $A(x) = 40x - 2x^2$. A downward-opening parabola with vertex at $(10, 200)$ and zeros at $x = 0$ and $x = 20$. A horizontal line $y = 150$ crosses the parabola at $x = 5$ and $x = 15$. The region between $x = 5$ and $x = 15$ where $A > 150$ shaded.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should see that the geometry, the algebra, and the inequality describe the same situation from three angles — and that the answer to "where is the area maximized" and "where does it exceed 150" both come from the same parabola.

**Style notes:** Clean two-panel layout. Width: full text-block.

## Figure 3 (candidate, not in current draft): The discriminant decision tree

**Placement:** Section 3, *Concept 2*, in the discriminant subsection.

**Description:** A simple decision diagram. Top: $b^2 - 4ac$. Three branches:

- $> 0$ → "two distinct real solutions"
- $= 0$ → "one repeated real solution"
- $< 0$ → "two complex solutions (conjugate pair)"

Each branch shows a small example quadratic and its solutions.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The student should see the discriminant as a quick preview of what the answer will look like.

**Style notes:** Decision-tree layout. Width: full text-block.
