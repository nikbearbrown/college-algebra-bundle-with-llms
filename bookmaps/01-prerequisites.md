## Deferred material

The chapter compresses a substantial amount of source material to keep within scope. Deferred:

- **Study-skills content from m51239 through m51248.** Each math source file is wrapped with an OpenStax Co-Requisite Skills lesson (study skills, growth mindset, Cornell note-taking, exam preparation, time management). These topics belong in a college-success companion or the existing `college-success-bundle` and are not part of the math content proper. The chapter does not reproduce them.
- **Math autobiography exercise from m51239.** A reflective writing exercise about past math experiences. Not reproduced in chapter; could be a first-day classroom activity in instructor materials.
- **The 37-question study-skills self-assessment from m51239.** Listed in pantry note above; deferred.
- **Detailed treatment of complex rational expressions from m51248.** Source has substantial worked examples of expressions like $\frac{1 + \frac{1}{x}}{2 - \frac{3}{x^2}}$. Compressed in chapter to a single paragraph; full treatment deferred to chapter 5 (Polynomial and Rational Functions).
- **Factoring expressions with fractional or negative exponents (m51247).** Source has examples like $x^{-1/2}(x + 1) + x^{1/2}$. Mentioned in pantry; deferred to a more advanced chapter.
- **Several conjugate-rationalization examples from m51242.** The chapter covers single-radical rationalization but defers binomial-conjugate rationalization (e.g., $\frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{2}}$) to chapter 6 (Exponential and Logarithmic Functions) where it appears in derivative/limit work.
- **Several specific numerical examples from each source file.** The chapter consolidates worked examples to keep the chapter readable; the full source-level practice exists for instructor use.

## Source-level notes

- **Authorship.** Sources are CNX-style module IDs (m51239 etc.) consistent with OpenStax-derived College Algebra material. Per workshop authorship rules, the rewrite is in standard third-person/second-person voice.
- **Source structure.** Each math source file (m51239 through m51248) is structured with a study-skills wrapper at the start (typically labeled "Co-Requisite Skills") followed by the math content. The chapter extracts the math content cleanly; production should not be confused by the study-skills sections in the originals.
- **Specific historical claims preserved from m51239.** The introduction of zero in fifth-century-CE India and negative numbers in seventh-century-CE India are historical claims the chapter reproduces. Production should verify against current standard math-history references (e.g., Boyer's *History of Mathematics*).
- **The Lebombo Bone (35,000 BCE) and Ishango Bone (20,000 BCE) references in m51240** are well-documented archaeological artifacts. Not used in chapter (compressed to a brief mention of mathematical artifacts in the chapter opening); could be expanded for production.
- **The Antarctic temperature record (−89.2°C) in m51240** — Vostok Station, July 21, 1983. The chapter preserves the record. Production should note that more recent satellite measurements have recorded lower surface temperatures (~−98°C), but those are surface readings rather than the WMO-recognized record. The chapter uses the canonical figure.
- **The garden integration example** in Concept 5 is built from chapter content rather than directly from a source example. The math is straightforward and source-faithful; the framing is added to provide a relatable application connecting all three concepts.
- **No internal contradictions** across the seven source files — they are designed to compose into one chapter.
